Gaza City

Gaza is a Palestinian coastal city, and the largest city in the Gaza Strip is located in its north,
at the southern tip of the eastern coast of the Mediterranean Sea. It is 78 km from the city of Jerusalem
to the southwest. It is the center of Gaza Governorate and the largest city of the Palestinian Authority in
terms of population. The population of Gaza Governorate reached 700,000 people in 2013, making it the largest
gathering of Palestinians in Palestine. It covers an area of ​​56 km2, making it one of the most densely populated
cities in the world.


Gaza City is considered one of the most important Palestinian cities, due to the importance of its strategic location and
the economic and urban importance of the city, in addition to being the temporary headquarters of the Palestinian National
Authority, and the presence of many of its headquarters and ministry in it.
